Risk Assessment

By applying mathematical and statistical models Oak Hamilton carries out risk and performance assessments of complex systems. By modelling a complex system and exploring how it behaves under different conditions consequences can be quantified and be used to inform system improvements and modifications. We take care to create a model which is accurate but not excessively complicated so that your resources are used effectively to produce maximal impact.

A risk assessment analyses systems in order to determine controls that will minimise the risks of damage or loss. Risk assessments may occur in many different contexts but they always require careful consideration of the constraints of a given system. When modelling a system, to understand its behaviour, it is important that any assumptions are understood and acknowledged. Performing a risk assessment gives a scientific basis for decisions that are made subsequently.

Mathematical modelling allows key processes and interactions to be quantified but is essential that the models are chosen carefully and the inherent assumptions are understood. Combining mathematical models with data analysis allows future scenarios to be predicted based upon certain system parameters. Different configurations can be tested in order to inform and optimise future decisions.

Oak Hamilton will help you identify and interpret the key outputs from your risk assessment so that you can make effective decisions. We will also quantify any uncertainty and test the sensitivity of the system to the parameters. Communication is key and we will present the outcomes of an assessment so that you understand how the results were determined. Model justification is something we take seriously; you need to be able to trust in the results we provide.
