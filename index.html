Examples

Spacecraft stability

Many processes which involve either small or large parameters can be modelled using asymptotic techniques. Problems in the domains of fluid mechanics, mechanical/biological systems and chemical reactions, amongst others, are routinely analysed using asymptotic methods. This example illustrates the basic techniques of asymptotic analysis by exploring the stability of an orbiting spacecraft to a small velocity disturbance.

Lattice-Boltzmann

Typically fluids are simulated by solving the Navier-Stokes equations numerically. An alternative approach is to use Lattice-Boltzmann methods to model particle propagation and collision on a discrete lattice. This approach can be particularly efficient on parallel architectures and handles complex geometries well. Here is a very simple simulation, implemented using the Rust programming language and WebAssembly, which can be run in a browser.

Convection-diffusion

How can we model the concentration of pollutants in a river, chemical concentrations in a pipe flow or chemotaxis observed in bacteria? The answer is the convection-diffusion equation. This equation describes how physical quantities are transferred due to diffusion and convection. Here is a simplified model that captures many of the salient features. The equation is solve numerically using a finite-difference scheme implemented in the Julia programming language.

About us

Oak Hamilton provides mathematical consultancy, research and software development services. It is our mission to solve real world problems in a timely and cost effective manner. We can provide your business with immediate assistance with imminent problems, continued assistance for ongoing issues or project based work for longer term business development.

Utilising scientific and computational technology is at the heart of what we do. By leveraging the latest research and practices we produce efficient solutions to complex problems. We aim to produce accurate and fast software based upon efficient algorithms and computational techniques. Your business requirements are our top priority so our solutions are based solely around what delivers the best outcome for you.

Oak Hamiltion was founded by Anthony O'Neill with the idea of using academic mathematical techniques to solve real world problems. Anthony has a PhD in applied mathematics from The University of Manchester where he has also worked as a researcher for a number of years. He has experience of working in the public sector having held positions at the Education and Skills Funding Agency and the Health and Safety Laboratory. He has also worked with BAE Systems on a long term mathematical modelling project.

Oak Hamilton is named after possibly the greatest Irish mathematician who ever lived, Sir William Rowan Hamilton. He is best known for his reformulation of Newtonian mechanics and as being the inventor of the quaternions. His work has been vital to the development of quantum mechanics and his ideas pervade modern physics. We hope to be worthy of his name.
