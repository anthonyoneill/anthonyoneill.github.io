Anthony O'Neill

I am an applied mathematician and software engineer with a passion for building accurate and efficient systems. I have a strong background in fluid mechanics, numerical methods, and scientific computing. I am skilled in a variety of programming languages and technologies, including Rust, C++ and Python.

Experience

  1. Senior Software Engineer Flite Software NI [ November 2020 - Present ]

    I currently work for Flite Software NI as a senior software engineer. My role involves developing mathematical models, algorithms and software for modelling transient fluid flow in complex pipe networks. I have devised a new algorithm for modelling transient flows in fluid networks using discrete calculus and Newton iteration, implemented it in Rust and deployed it to an Actix Web server. At the moment I am working to improve system by implementing additional components and including convective effects in the governing equations.

  2. Research Associate University of Manchester [ November 2018 - November 2020 ]

    I worked in the School of Engineering investigating quantum numerical algorithms for use in simulating physical systems. The project aimed to identify and modify numerical algorithms for use with quantum computing systems with particular regard for the modelling and simulation of fluids. I worked as part of the smoothed particle hydrodynamics group where I presented updates on my research in weekly team meetings. I lead a course on numerical methods and computing for undergraduate civil engineering students. Participated in conferences (SPHERIC 2019 and SPH@Manchester 2020).

    • Williams, A.J. and Lind, S.J., 2020. A Quantum Computing Algorithm for Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics. arXiv preprint arXiv:2006.06719

    • Au-Yeung, R., Williams, A., Kendon, V. and Lind, S., Quantum Algorithm for Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics. Computer Physics Communications, 2024. Volume 294, 108909.

  3. Data Analyst Education and Skills Funding Agency [ September 2018 - November 2018 ]

    Working for an executive agency of the Department of Education I helped develop data models and risk assesment tools for determining funding and financial risk using R and PowerBI.

  4. PhD in Applied Mathematics University of Manchester [ September 2015 - September 2018 ]

    I worked on a PhD project investigating boundary layers in three-dimensional parabolic flows. The project used analytic, asymptotic and computational techniques to assess the impact of surface deviations or wall transpiration on boundary layer development. I Developed a libraray of code (C++), based on Rich Hewitt's library CppNoddy, for the numerical solution of equations modelling fluid flow including both large sparse linear systems and eigenvalue problems (using PETSc and SLEPc). I used Python and Matplotlib extensively for data analysis and visualisation.

    • Hewitt, R.E., Duck, P.W. and Williams, A.J., 2017. Injection into boundary layers: solutions beyond the classical form. Journal of Fluid Mechanics, 822, pp.617-639.

    • Williams, A.J. and Hewitt, R.E., 2017. Micro-slot injection into a boundary layer driven by a favourable pressure gradient. Journal of Engineering Mathematics, 107(1), pp.19-35.

    • Williams, A.J., 2018. Boundary-layer flows with short-scale spanwise disturbances. The University of Manchester (United Kingdom)

  5. Risk Assessment Scientist Health and Safety Laboratory [ April 2015 - September 2015 ]

    I developed gas dispersion modelling tools as part of the Mathematical Sciences Unit. I developed a number of coded solutions using Visual Basic and C++ for use in risk assessments. I created, coded and tested mathematical models for weather conditions and water reactive substances.

  6. MSc in Applied Mathematics with Industrial Modelling University of Manchester [ September 2013 - September 2014 ]

I developed a range of skills for mathematical modelling through modules in Stability Theory, Continuum Mechanics, Mathematical Methods and Non-linear Waves. I was Nominated for postgraduate student of the year within the faculty of Engineering and Physical Sciences.
